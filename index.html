I am a PhD student in Geotechnical Engineering at the University of Trento (UNITN), Italy. My doctoral research deals with the numerical simulation of active clay under chemo-mechanical loading for prediction of landslide movement. During my PhD, I developed a 2D element in user element subroutine (UEL) of ABAQUS in order to simulate the coupling between displacement, flow of water and diffusion of salt in soil. In order to validate the model, the experimental oedometer test on an active clay has been simulated by using this element. The preliminary result of this research have been presented in ALERT workshop. Furthermore, we proposed a new elasticity law to simulate the natural soil in adition to active caly. The simulation results have been validated with new experimental results. We are preparing some papers to publish in conference and journal. In addition, I am developing another element in user element subroutine (UEL) of ABAQUS to simulated discontinuity in the soil in which the flow of water and salt diffusion have been considered.

Chemo-mechanical modelling of active clay

Consolidation and swelling of clay occurs by changing the chemical composition of pore water in addition to mechanical load. Experimental results of oedometer test on active clay have shown that by increasing the salinity of water, under a constant load, the volume of clay decreased. This volume decrease has been called chemical consolidation. On the other hand, when the salty water is replaced with distilled water, the volume increases which is called chemical swelling.

Consolidation

Experimental results of oedometer test on active clay have shown that there are two ways to consolidate an active clay:
1) mechanical loading
2) increasing the salinity of water

Swelling

Experimental results of oedometer test on active clay have shown that there are two ways for an active clay to swell:
1) mechanical unloading
2) decreasing the salinity of water

Simulation of chemo-mechanical coupling

In order to validate the 2d element defined in user element subroutine (UEL) of ABAQUS, an experimental oedometer test has been simulated. For simplicity in the simulation, only half of the sample with one porous stone at the bottom has been considered. For visualizing the results of a user defined element, we use a domestic special routines developed by someone else.

Salt concentration during chemical consolidation

In this video, it is shown the diffusion of salt from bottom of the porous stone inside the clay. As it can be seen the salt concentration increases in the bottom and this increase propagates inside the porous stone and clay.

Salt concentration during chemical swelling

In this video, it is shown the flow of water from bottom of the porous stone inside the clay. As it can be seen the salt concentration decreases in the bottom and this decrease propagates inside the porous stone and clay.

Pore pressure during chemical consolidation

In this video, it is shown the change of pore pressure during chemical consolidation. At the begining of consolidation the negative pore pressure occurs at the bottom of the sample which propagates in the sample. After negative pore pressure dissipates, the pore pressure increases until it vanishes at the end of chemical consolidation.

Pore pressure during chemical swelling

In this video, it is shown the change of pore pressure during chemical swelling. As it can be seen, there is negative pore pressure at the begining of swelling but the magnitude is smalrer compared to consolidation.

Mass of absorbed water during chemical consolidation

In this video, it is shown the change of mass of absorbed water in the sample. As it can be seen, by increasing the salt concentration from the bottom of the sample, mass of absorbed water decreases from the bottom to the middle of the sample.

Mass of absorbed water during chemical swelling

In this video, it is shown the change of mass of absorbed water in the sample. As it can be seen, by decreasing the salt concentration from the bottom of the sample, mass of absorbed water increases from the bottom to the middle of the sample.
